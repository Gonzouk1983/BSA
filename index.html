The Iconic Motorcycle & Sidecar

As seen in Dad's Army & On The Buses

Historical Info

This is a BSA m21 600cc 1949 (JVB54) motorcycle with sidecar.

Vehicle Details

Model: BSA M21

Engine: 600cc side-valve single-cylinder

The BSA M21 was renowned for its durability and torque, making it a popular choice for both civilian and military applications during and after World War II. Its robust construction and torque-rich engine made it ideal for sidecar applications, which is likely why it was selected for use in period dramas and films.
